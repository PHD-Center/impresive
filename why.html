Why international multi-database studies

Not simply pooling data from many countries — but answering one question consistently across very different settings.

Definition

What is an international multi-database study?

It is not the simple merging of data from several countries. It is answering the same disease or treatment question — with a consistent research question, a shared analytic logic and a transparent method — across different healthcare systems, data structures and population backgrounds.

The value

Why cross-national collaboration matters

Power for rare events

Increase sample size and event counts to study rare diseases or rare adverse events.

Compare across systems

Contrast different healthcare systems and prescribing contexts.

Test transportability

Assess whether findings transfer and remain valid across settings.

Support decisions

Inform regulatory, HTA, clinical and policy decisions with multi-country evidence.

Privacy-preserving

Collaborate internationally without transferring raw personal data.

Rapid response

Build the readiness to respond quickly to public-health and treatment questions.

Fit-for-purpose

When is a cross-national study the right choice?

✔ Well suited when…

  • Event counts in a single country are insufficient
  • Multi-country comparison is needed
  • Extrapolation and transportability must be assessed
  • Post-marketing safety surveillance is required
  • Studying new drugs, rare diseases or special populations
  • Rapid multi-country RWE is needed to support a decision
  • Comparing disease burden or outcomes across treatment models or health systems

✘ Often not suited when…

  • The question is purely a single-country policy matter
  • Variable definitions are not yet clear
  • Data quality differs too greatly between countries
  • Exposure, outcome or time-zero cannot be defined consistently
  • Local analytic and governance capacity is lacking
