About IMPRESIVE

Why the platform exists, what its name means, and the philosophy that holds it together.

The challenge

Single databases can no longer answer the hardest questions alone

As real-world data and real-world evidence become central to drug safety, treatment effectiveness, disease burden, post-marketing surveillance and reimbursement decisions, single-country or single-database studies increasingly fall short.

Countries differ in their healthcare systems, data structures, disease burden, prescribing habits, population characteristics and data-governance rules. Cross-national collaboration is therefore not only about increasing sample size — it is how we test whether findings are consistent, transportable and useful for decisions.

But collaboration runs into real obstacles: privacy constraints, inconsistent methods, incompatible data formats, difficulties in cross-border extrapolation, and the challenge of interpreting results that diverge between settings.

The response

The birth of IMPRESIVE

IMPRESIVE was created to make cross-national real-world evidence reproducible, comparable, transportable and decision-ready. It does this through international collaboration, database standardization, distributed analysis and results visualization — so that data from different countries can answer the same question quickly, transparently and reproducibly, without ever moving raw individual-level records.

IMPRESIVE is not a one-off study. It is a platform that connects data preparedness, method preparedness, analysis preparedness and decision preparedness into one framework.

Core philosophy

More than collecting data

IMPRESIVE is not about gathering data. It is about building a platform where different countries can use a common language, common methods and a common visualization framework to answer questions about disease and treatment — so that data is not merely compared, but jointly forms evidence that is more reliable, more transportable, and genuinely able to support decisions.
