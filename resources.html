Bias demonstrations

Hands-on examples of the biases that most often threaten real-world evidence.

Immortal time bias

Mis-handled follow-up time during which the outcome cannot occur, artificially favoring the "treated" group. The demo shows how time-zero definition and target-trial emulation fix it.

Confounding by indication

Treatment is chosen because of disease severity, mixing the reason for treatment with its effect. The demo contrasts naïve vs. confounding-adjusted estimates.

Protopathic bias

A drug given for early symptoms of an as-yet-undiagnosed disease looks falsely causal. The demo shows the effect of exposure lag windows.

Misclassification

Errors in exposure or outcome definitions bias results. The demo varies code-based phenotype accuracy to show the impact.

Selection bias

Who enters or stays in the cohort distorts the comparison. The demo illustrates differential enrolment and loss to follow-up.

Transportability problem

A valid estimate in one population may not transfer to another with a different case-mix. The demo reweights to a target population.

Cross-country heterogeneity

Real differences between health systems, not error, drive divergent results. The demo separates true heterogeneity from artefact.
